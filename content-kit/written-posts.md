# 10 written posts — X / LinkedIn / Threads (faceless brand account)

Post 3–4/week. Same brand voice as the videos: specific, numeric, generous, zero guru-speak.

---

**1.** Audited a Shopify store yesterday. 214 five-star reviews. Zero visible above the
fold. Moved one widget, added a star badge next to the title. That's the whole industry
secret: your best marketing is usually already on your site, hidden.

**2.** The most expensive sentence in e-commerce is the one that isn't there:
"Free shipping over $45." Buyers finding a $6.95 surprise at checkout is still the #1
abandonment trigger, and it's a 10-minute fix.

**3.** Your Shopify conversion checklist, shortest version I can write:
price visible → shipping stated → reviews near title → sticky mobile ATC → express pay on
→ guest checkout → one clear hero CTA. Seven checks. Most stores fail four.

**4.** Unpopular audit take: your store doesn't need another app. Of the last 10 stores
we scored, 8 needed SETTINGS changed, not software added. Apps are how you slow your
site down avoiding the settings menu.

**5.** "We need more traffic" — the most common thing store owners say, and the most
common misdiagnosis. 5,400 sessions at 1.6% conversion isn't a traffic problem. It's a
leaky bucket with a marketing budget.

**6.** Fake urgency timers don't just fail — they inoculate. Once a visitor sees your
countdown reset, every real claim on your site inherits the doubt. Honest scarcity or
none.

**7.** Case note (anonymized, with consent): candle store, $2.9k/mo. Three fixes — sticky
ATC, shipping threshold, review placement. Conservative math: +$540–$1,350/mo. Time
required: under two hours. The fixes are boring. That's why they were still available.

**8.** If your homepage headline is "Welcome to our store," your store's first impression
is that nobody finished it. What you sell, who it's for, why you — one sentence. Rent
that top pixel row to nothing else.

**9.** Mobile test you can run in 60 seconds: open your best-seller on your phone. Scroll
to the description like a curious buyer. Now — can you still see a buy button? If not,
you're making 70% of your visitors scroll UP to give you money.

**10.** We score every store 0–100 across 47 checkpoints. The scores cluster at 50–69:
"leaking." Almost nobody is broken. Almost nobody is optimized. Which means for most
stores the next $1k/mo is already on the site, mispriced at zero.
