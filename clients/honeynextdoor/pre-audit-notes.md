# Honey Next Door — practice client #1 (pre-audit notes)

Store: https://honeynextdoor.com · Atlanta, GA · hyperlocal honey (neighborhood hives:
Buckhead, Candler Park, Oakhurst, Druid Hills, Inman Park, VA-Highland, Brookhaven,
Morningside, Pittsburgh, Sandy Springs, EAV, Grant Park, Decatur) + soap, beeswax
candles, lip balm. Georgia Grown member. Confirmed Shopify.

## Signals observable from indexed data ONLY (not yet a scored audit)

1. **Homepage title tag looks broken:** indexed as "Honey Next Door – Natural Honey From
   Local Atlanta Bees - Tupelo Honey" — a product name appears appended to the homepage
   title. SEO + first-impression fix, likely minutes to correct. VERIFY on live site.
2. **Generic product titles:** `/products/soap` ("Soap"), `/products/candles`
   ("Candles") — single generic listings, not benefit-led titles (checklist pt 12), and
   likely one listing with variants instead of well-named products. Hurts SEO + CR.
3. **Legacy `frontpage` collection indexed** ("Home page – Honey Next Door") — default
   Shopify scaffolding visible to Google; suggests limited SEO housekeeping overall.
4. **Strong differentiator, probably under-merchandised:** neighborhood-specific hives
   is a moat ("honey from YOUR street") — check whether the store actually sells
   neighborhood-labeled jars, subscriptions, or gift positioning around it.
5. Catalog spans honey varieties (Tupelo, Sourwood, Sweet Clover, Creamed, Spicy,
   Bourbon Barrel, Gallberry) + body care — bundle/gift-box potential is obvious
   (checklist pt 40) — verify whether any bundles exist.

## Growth brief: the Savannah Bee path, translated

Savannah Bee Company (Savannah, GA — same-state case study): boutiques → Fancy Food
Show 2003 → wholesale (Williams-Sonoma, Whole Foods, 1000+ retailers, ~150 SKUs) →
own retail from 2008 (now ~16 stores) → product-line expansion into health & beauty
(+545% category growth 2012–16 vs +164% overall) → experiential retail.

Honey Next Door translation, in order of nearest money:
1. **Own the hyperlocal wedge online** — neighborhood-labeled jars as the hero product;
   "honey from your neighborhood" gifting; per-neighborhood landing pages (SEO: "Buckhead
   honey" etc.).
2. **Subscriptions** — seasonal honey club (their varieties rotate naturally).
3. **B2B gifting** — Atlanta realtors (closing gift: "honey from the neighborhood you
   just moved into") + corporate welcome boxes. Small outreach, recurring orders.
4. **Host-a-hive corporate sponsorships** — companies sponsor a hive, get branded jars
   (recurring B2B revenue; they already manage distributed hives).
5. **Wholesale via Georgia Grown network** → local grocers (they're already a member) →
   then the Fancy Food Show route when ops are ready.
6. **Body-care line depth** (SBC's fastest-growing category) — they already make soap,
   lip balm; expand deliberately after DTC conversion is fixed.

Sequence matters: fix the store's conversion first (the audit), THEN pour the brand
moves into a site that converts.
