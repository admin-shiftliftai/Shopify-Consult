## Email v1 — the "I found a problem you'll want to know about" open (recommended)

**Subject:** quick heads-up on your elderberry syrup listing

Hi — I'm a local Atlanta person and a Honey Next Door customer (the Inman Park creamed
honey is dangerous, in a good way).

I do Shopify conversion work, and while I was on your site I noticed two things on the
Elderberry Syrup page you'll probably want to fix quickly — not conversion nitpicks, real
issues:

1. The description makes medical claims ("clinically shown to… lessen the severity and
   length of your illness"). For a food product that's the kind of wording the FDA/FTC
   send warning letters over — worth softening.
2. There's a leftover eBay tracking pixel pasted into the description HTML (an
   `<img>` from rover.ebay.com) — it's firing a third-party tracker on your page and
   doing nothing for you.

Both are ~15-minute fixes. Happy to send you the exact replacement text if useful — no
charge, I just like the brand and hate seeing a good store carry avoidable risk.

Separately: I put together a short list of a few other things I noticed (your neighborhood
honey is your best asset and it's basically hidden from Google). If you'd ever want the
full walkthrough, I do a fixed-price conversion audit — but genuinely, fix the elderberry
page regardless.
