Run your first AI store audit

No installing. No code. You'll do the whole thing in your web browser in about 15 minutes — this walks you through every click and shows you what each screen looks like.

Where it runs

Two browser tabs, that's it. Tab 1: the store you're auditing. Tab 2: claude.ai — a free Claude account, the same kind of chat you're talking to right now. You copy a few things from Tab 1, paste them into Tab 2, and Claude writes the report. Nothing gets downloaded to your computer except the finished PDF.

⏱ ~15 min per audit 💵 Cost: $0 (free Claude account) 🧰 You need: Chrome + claude.ai 🎯 Do 2–3 practice stores first
1

Pick a store to audit

2 minutes

For practice, grab any small Shopify store. Easiest source: scroll Instagram or TikTok until you hit a product ad, tap through to the store. Stores running ads have real traffic — which is exactly who needs an audit (and who'll pay for one).

What you'll have open in Tab 1

https://ember-and-oak.com

How to tell

It's a Shopify store if adding /products.json to the end of the web address (next step) shows a wall of text instead of an error. Most product brands you see advertised are on Shopify.

2

Copy the store's product list

2 minutes

  1. Click the web address bar at the top of the browser.
  2. Type the store address followed by /products.json and press Enter — e.g. ember-and-oak.com/products.json
  3. You'll see a screen full of code-looking text. Don't panic — you don't read it. Just select all of it (⌘/Ctrl + A) and copy it (⌘/Ctrl + C).

What you'll see (the highlighted part = selected & copied)

ember-and-oak.com/products.json
{ "products": [ { "title": "Cedar Smoke Candle 8oz", "price": "28.00", "images": 4 }, { "title": "Vanilla Oak Candle 8oz", "price": "28.00", "images": 3 }, { "title": "Wick Trimmer", "price": "12.00", "images": 1 } ] }
That's it

This text is just the store's product list — names, prices, how many photos each has. Claude reads it so it can judge the catalog. You've now got it on your clipboard; hold onto it for Step 5.

3

Screenshot two pages

3 minutes

Take a picture of the screen for (a) the store's homepage and (b) one product page (click any product first). Save both to your desktop — you'll attach them in Step 5.

Mac⌘+Shift+4then drag
Windows⊞ Win+Shift+Sthen drag
Pro move

Also snap the product page on your phone — most store traffic is mobile, and half your findings live there (like a buy button that scrolls away). One extra screenshot, much sharper audit.

5

Paste the prompt, attach screenshots, hit Send

2 minutes

  1. Open the file audit-engine/prompts/00-one-shot-simple.md and copy everything in it.
  2. Paste it into the Claude box. Fill in the store's web address where it says {{PASTE STORE URL}}, and paste your product-list text (from Step 2) where it says {{PASTE THE JSON}}.
  3. Click the 📎 paperclip and attach your two (or three) screenshots.
  4. Press Send.

7

Fact-check every finding

5 minutes — this is the part that makes it worth $297

Put the store and the report side by side. For every issue Claude names, confirm you can actually see it on the real store. If you can't find it, delete that line. AI is fast but occasionally confident and wrong — you are the quality check, and that's exactly what the client is paying for.

Rule

Nothing ships in a paid report unless you've seen it with your own eyes on the live store. Screenshot each of your top-3 findings so the client can see it too.

8

Save as PDF — done

2 minutes

  • Practice run: just keep it. Turn the juiciest finding into your first teardown video (see content-kit/).
  • Paid run: paste the cleaned report into a Google Doc → File → Download → PDF, then email it to the client.
